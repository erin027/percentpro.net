Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate sales tax?

Multiply the price by the tax rate divided by 100: Tax = Price × (Rate ÷ 100).

Example: $50 item at 8% tax: $50 × 0.08 = $4.00 tax. Total = $50 + $4 = $54.00.

How do you find the price before tax?

Divide the total (tax-inclusive) price by (1 + tax rate): Pre-tax = Total ÷ (1 + Rate/100).

Example: Total is $54 with 8% tax: $54 ÷ 1.08 = $50.00.

What is the difference between marginal and effective tax rate?

Your marginal tax rate is the rate applied to your last dollar of income — the highest bracket you fall into. Your effective tax rate is the average rate across all your income, calculated by dividing total tax paid by total income.

Example: If your marginal rate is 22% but your effective rate is 14%, you're only actually paying 14 cents in tax for every dollar earned overall.

What states have no sales tax?

Five US states have no statewide sales tax: Oregon, Montana, New Hampshire, Delaware, and Alaska. However, Alaska allows local jurisdictions to impose sales taxes, so some Alaskan cities do charge sales tax.

What is VAT and how does it differ from sales tax?

VAT (Value Added Tax) is collected at every stage of production and distribution, with each business paying tax on the value they add. Sales tax is collected only at the final point of sale to the consumer. Both result in a similar final price to the consumer, but VAT is used in most countries outside the US, while sales tax is the US system.

About this calculator

This tax calculator has three modes: calculate sales tax on a purchase, reverse-calculate the pre-tax price from a total, and estimate US federal income tax using current 2024 tax brackets. The income tax estimate is for federal tax only and does not include state income tax, FICA, or deductions.

For professional tax advice, consult a qualified tax professional or CPA. This tool is for estimation purposes only.
