Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about calculating discounts, sale prices, and savings.

How do you calculate a discount?

Multiply the original price by the discount percentage divided by 100: Discount = Price × (Discount% ÷ 100). Then subtract from the original price to get the sale price.

Example: 20% off $80: $80 × 0.20 = $16 off. Sale price = $80 − $16 = $64.

How do you find the original price before a discount?

Divide the sale price by (1 minus the discount rate): Original = Sale price ÷ (1 − Discount%/100).

Example: Item costs $64 after 20% off. $64 ÷ 0.80 = $80 original price.

How do you calculate the discount percentage?

Subtract the sale price from the original, divide by the original, multiply by 100: Discount% = ((Original − Sale) ÷ Original) × 100.

Example: $80 → $64. ((80 − 64) ÷ 80) × 100 = 20% off.

How do stacked or double discounts work?

Stacked discounts are applied sequentially, not added together. A 20% discount followed by a 10% discount is not 30% off.

Example: $100 item with 20% off then 10% off. Step 1: $100 × 0.80 = $80. Step 2: $80 × 0.90 = $72. Total saving = $28 (28%), not 30%.

What is the difference between a discount and a rebate?

A discount reduces the price at the point of sale — you pay less upfront. A rebate is a partial refund after purchase — you pay full price and receive money back later, often by mail or as a statement credit. Rebates are common in electronics and automotive purchases.
