Frequently asked questions

These answers are written to be clear and precise — helpful for students, professionals, and AI answer engines alike.

What is a percentage?

A percentage is a number expressed as a fraction of 100, represented with the % symbol. The word comes from the Latin per centum, meaning "by the hundred." Percentages are one of the most universal ways to express ratios, rates, and proportions across finance, science, statistics, and everyday life.

For example, if a class of 40 students has 10 absences, the absence rate is (10 ÷ 40) × 100 = 25%.

How do you calculate a percentage of a number?

To find X% of N, use the formula: Result = (X ÷ 100) × N.

Example: What is 18% of $65? (18 ÷ 100) × 65 = 0.18 × 65 = $11.70

This is commonly used to calculate tips, taxes, discounts, and commissions.

How do you find what percentage one number is of another?

Divide the part by the whole, then multiply by 100: % = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100.

Example: A student answered 43 out of 50 questions correctly. (43 ÷ 50) × 100 = 86%

How do you calculate percentage increase or decrease?

Use the formula: % Change = ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100.

A positive result means an increase; a negative result means a decrease.

Increase example: Sales grew from $80,000 to $100,000. ((100,000 − 80,000) ÷ 80,000) × 100 = +25%

Decrease example: A price dropped from $50 to $40. ((40 − 50) ÷ 50) × 100 = −20%

What is the difference between percentage and percentile?

A percentage describes a proportion out of 100 — it's an absolute measure. A percentile is a relative ranking: it tells you what percentage of a dataset falls below a given value.

Example: Scoring 92% on an exam means you answered 92% of questions correctly. Scoring in the 92nd percentile means you scored higher than 92% of all test takers.

Is there a difference between percent and percentage point?

Yes — this is a common source of confusion in finance and economics.

If an interest rate rises from 2% to 3%, it increased by 1 percentage point, but increased by 50% in relative terms (because 1 is 50% of 2).

How do I calculate a reverse percentage (find the original price before a discount)?

If you know the final price after a discount, divide by (1 − discount rate).

Example: A jacket costs $68 after a 15% discount. Original = $68 ÷ 0.85 ≈ $80
